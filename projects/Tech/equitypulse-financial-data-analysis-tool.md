# EquityPulse: Financial Data Analysis Tool

**EquityPulse** is a fundamentals-first financial intelligence platform designed to make equity research accessible and easy to understand, even for retail investors without a formal finance background. By cutting out the noise and boilerplate disclosures, EquityPulse provides structured, high-value insights directly from official company filings and reports.

Currently, the platform hosts data for **70+ companies**, containing **39+ annual reports**, **30+ credit reports**, **55+ transcripts**, and **51+ company analyses**, serving an average of **1,000+ active users**.

---

## The Motivation

For retail investors, extracting meaningful information from corporate filings presents significant hurdles:
1. **Terminology Barrier**: Technical jargon makes documents hard to interpret.
2. **Information Overload**: Important insights are buried within 400-to-500 page PDFs containing generic legal language.
3. **High Subscription Costs**: Traditional platforms either gate detailed analyses behind expensive paywalls or lag in providing updated insights (even following mid-2026 plan additions).

EquityPulse is built to deliver high-quality, actionable insights directly from primary sources, facilitating institutional-grade research without unnecessary noise.

---

## Core Product Offerings

EquityPulse organizes corporate insights into four key sections, helping users quickly understand a company's financial story.

### Platform Structure & Offerings

* **Analysis**: Company-wise in-depth analysis of the annual report.
* **Annual Reports**: Provides the summary of the annual report in one page.
* **Credit Reports**: Inside of the credit reports.
* **Conference Calls**: One page inside from the conference call PDF.

### Core Areas of Analysis

Each report filters out noise to focus on the key operational and financial health metrics:

* **Business Quality**: Evaluates pricing power, competitive moats, and customer concentration.
* **Revenue Quality**: Breaks down recurring vs. one-off revenue and segment performance.
* **Profit Margins**: Reviews margin stability and operational leverage.
* **Cash Conversion**: Audits the working capital cycle and CFO-to-EBITDA ratio.
* **Capital Efficiency**: Tracks RoE (Return on Equity) and RoCE (Return on Capital Employed).

## Lessons Learned

* **Targeted Extraction**: Standard corporate filings contain a massive ratio of noise to signal. By targeting specific financial quality dimensions, we can isolate core fundamentals.
* **Democratizing Finance**: Structuring the qualitative aspects (like management discussion and credit rating notes) into a concise layout provides better clarity than raw financial numbers alone.
